A brief history of the 10 Canadian provinces and 3 territories

On July 1, 1867, Canada was divided into the four original provinces: Ontario, Quebec, New Brunswick, and Nova Scotia. In the New Dominion in 1870, the Northwest Territories and Manitoba joined the federation, then British Columbia in 1871. Yukon came next when it was separated from the Northwest Territories in 1898. Prince Edward Island became the seventh, smallest, and least populated province in 1873. Later on in 1905, Saskatchewan and Alberta were created, completing what we know as western Canada today. Newfoundland became the 10th province in 1949. Nunavut was separated from the the Northwest Territories on April 1, 1999, forming the northern most territory of Canada.
